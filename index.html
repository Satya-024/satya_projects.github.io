About K-Maps

A Karnaugh Map (K-Map) is a visual tool for simplifying Boolean algebra expressions. It reduces the complexity of digital circuits by identifying groups of adjacent cells containing 1s, which represent prime implicants. The groups must have powers of 2 sizes (1, 2, 4, or 8 cells).

Key Rules:

  • Groups must contain 1s that are adjacent horizontally or vertically
  • Group size must be a power of 2 (1, 2, 4, 8, or 16)
  • The top/bottom and left/right edges wrap around (toroidal)
  • Overlap of groups is allowed
